CASE SUMMARY

This case involves a patient who suffered severe injuries after an allergic reaction to amoxicillin. On March 8, 2012, patient presented to clinic for a surgical procedure. Her preoperative paper work clearly stated that she had an allergy to penicillin. Post operatively, she was prescribed amoxicillin by defendant. On March 8, 201, she presented to the drug store and filled the prescription for amoxicillin. Read More

The allergy information listed on her pharmacy records included penicillin. Nevertheless, amoxicillin was dispensed to defendant. On March 10, 2012, patient presented to hospital with an anaphylactic reaction to the amoxicillin prescribed and dispensed by defendants. On April 25, 2012, patient presented to a neurological surgeon with complaints of severe headaches , tunnel vision, floaters in her eyes and flashing lights in her peripheral vision and vertigo type symptoms following an allergic reaction to amoxicillin.

An MRI demonstrated a pineal glad cyst. The neurosurgeon stated that her allergic reaction to amoxicillin either caused the pineal glad lesion to become inflamed or expansile producing aqueductal stenosis and symptoms of obstructive hydrocephalus. She continues to have severe, daily headaches, tunnel vision with floaters. dizziness, short term memory loss, difficulty sleeping, decreased cognitive function and new onset of multiple allergies and asthma. It is alleged that amoxicillin should not have been dispensed to patient being that she had a known allergy for penicillin. It is also claimed that the patient should have been informed that ingesting such a medication could cause a fatal anaphylactic reaction. Read Less
